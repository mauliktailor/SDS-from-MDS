Introduction:
Here are some tornado facts from the National Weather Service,Insurance Information Institute and news accounts:   _
Tornadoes can occur in any month, but are more frequent fromApril through June and between 3 and 6 p.m.
_
Most tornadoes track southwest to northeast, but their pathscan spiral erratically.
_
There were 32 tornado-related deaths reported in 1988, downfrom 59 in 1987 and well below the average of 99 a year.
_Less than 2 percent of all tornadoes are classified as violent,with wind speeds of more than 200 mph and a path averaging 26miles.
_
The largest single outbreak of twisters on record was in April1974, when 148 storms killed 300 people in 13 states over two days.
