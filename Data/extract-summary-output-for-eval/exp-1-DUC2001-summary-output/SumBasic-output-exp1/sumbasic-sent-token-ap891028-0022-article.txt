The Richter scale is a measure of ground motion as recorded onseismographs.
Every increase of one number means a tenfold increasein magnitude.
Thus a reading of 7.5 reflects an earthquake 10 timesstronger than one of 6.5.
A 7 reading is a ``major'' earthquake, capable ofwidespread heavy damage; 8 is a ``great'' quake, capable oftremendous damage.
