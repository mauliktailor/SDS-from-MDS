Abstract:Firefighters in California, Michigan, Montana, Wyoming and Utah battled holiday weekend fires that blackened more than 6,000 acres of forest and wilderness areas.
Illegal fireworks were blamed for a blaze that swept over 2,200 acres of Stanislaus National Forest in California.
In southern Utah's scenic Zion National Park, firefighters tried to contain a 600-acre fire that was ignited by lightening.
Meanwhile, three-quarters of an inch of rain on Monday helped slow a fire on 1,650 acres in the Shoshone National Forest in Wyoming.
A fire which burned 8,700 acres in Arizona's Tonto National Forest was reported extinguished on Monday.
